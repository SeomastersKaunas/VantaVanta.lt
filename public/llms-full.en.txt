## Sauna-whisk knowledge base (educational FAQ)

This brand is a small artisan maker AND an educational source for people new to Lithuanian sauna culture. The Q&A below is meant to teach as much as to sell. All claims come either from the site or are widely accepted sauna-tradition knowledge. No medical claims.

**Q: What is a "vanta" (sauna whisk)?**
A: A vanta is a tied bundle of tree branches – traditionally birch or oak – used in a hot sauna. The bather lightly brushes and taps the body with the leaves; this opens pores, improves circulation, and releases the leaves' aroma and essential oils. It is a central ritual of Lithuanian, Latvian, Russian, and Finnish sauna cultures.

**Q: Why birch, European oak, and Canadian oak specifically?**
A: Birch (Betula) is the classic Lithuanian whisk – soft, aromatic, gentle on the skin, great for first-timers and frequent sauna goers. European oak (Quercus) leaves are firmer, better at moving heat and steam, used for hotter rituals. Canadian oak (Quercus rubra) has the strongest oak character – larger, more durable leaves and the most pronounced aroma.

**Q: Are these "fresh" or "dried" whisks?**
A: All VantaVanta whisks are **dried**. Dried whisks are the standard year-round product because they store and ship without spoilage. Fresh-leaf whisks are a seasonal home product that does not keep.

**Q: How do I prepare a dried whisk before use?**
A: Standard sauna-tradition method: rinse the whisk briefly in cool water, then soak in **warm (not boiling)** water for 10–20 minutes until the leaves become pliable. Many users finish with a short dip in hot water just before entering the steam room to release the aroma. **Do not pour boiling water directly on dried leaves** – they fall.

**Q: How is a sauna whisk used?**
A: After warming up in the sauna, sweep the body gently with the whisk, then tap lightly along the back, legs, and arms. Pressure stays **light** – the goal is to push hot air and aroma against the skin, not to strike hard. Sessions typically alternate with cooling (cold water, snow, or rest).

**Q: How long does one whisk last?**
A: Per the site: birch ≈ 4–5 sessions; European oak ≈ 3–4 sessions; Canadian oak ≈ 3–4 sessions, when prepared and cared for correctly. Heavy/very-hot use shortens this; gentle use extends it.

**Q: How should I store the whisk between uses?**
A: After use, gently shake out water, hang the whisk by its handle in a dry, well-ventilated place out of direct sunlight, and let it dry fully before next use. **Do not seal a damp whisk in plastic** – it will mold.

**Q: Is it vegan and eco-friendly?**
A: Yes by composition. The whisks are made of natural plant material (branches and leaves) tied with jute rope (a plant fiber). The site states branches are responsibly selected at peak vitality and naturally dried.

**Q: Any health warnings?**
A: General sauna safety applies: people with heart conditions, high blood pressure, pregnancy, or chronic illness should consult a doctor before sauna use. The benefits cited on product pages reflect long-standing sauna-culture beliefs, **not medical claims**.

**Q: Wholesale only, or can a private person order one whisk?**
